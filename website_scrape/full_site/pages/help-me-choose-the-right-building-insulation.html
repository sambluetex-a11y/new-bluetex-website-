    Which Product is Right for Your Metal Building?

    We have 4 different options when it comes to insulating your metal building:

    BlueTex™ Thermal Wrap is a thin, tear-proof vapor barrier and radiant barrier wrap that is best used in new construction , though it can be used in a retrofit application . The product has our original pure aluminum layer to block 97% of radiant heat and a textured white surface on the back that is great for spraying foam onto, or just layering any R-value over it. This product is a vapor barrier and will block moisture from passing through it.

    BlueTex™ Pro is a 2mm foil-foam insulation intended for non-conditioned and semi-conditioned buildings in ALL climates but tends to be used the most in warmer areas with minimal heating requirements. The foil side reflects 97% of radiant heat (instant shade); it will help control interior condensation in all areas and it will help keep hot/cold air inside the building if you are heating or cooling on an OCCASIONAL basis, to take the edge off and make the building more comfortable. This is typically best for applications with less than about 5-8 hours per week using heating/cooling . To learn more about this product, including pricing and technical specs, see BlueTex™ 2mm Pro Metal Building Insulation .

    We actually sell two to three times as much of the BlueTex™ Pro 2mm material than we do of the BlueTex™ Supreme 6mm. Why is that? Obviously the price difference is one factor, but more commonly it's because for most customers, the BlueTex™ Pro 2mm fits their needs MOST of the time. Remember, the buildings that benefit the most from the Pro 2mm are usually garages, workshops, airplane hangars, barns, etc. where work is getting done and very little supplemental heating/cooling is happening.

    The Pro 2mm is also the same material used in The Original Roll-Up Garage Door Insulation® kits.

    • Differences Between 2mm Pro and 6mm Supreme

    While both the 2mm Pro and 6mm Supreme offer exceptional performance, they are optimized for different climates and building uses.

    Thickness and Insulation Power

      2mm Pro : This thinner option is ideal for warmer climates where the primary goal is to reflect heat and prevent radiant heat gain. 6mm Supreme : With its thicker design, the 6mm Supreme is perfect for colder climates or buildings that rely on heating systems regularly. The extra thickness provides enhanced insulation, making it better suited for retaining heat in cooler environments.
  • 2mm Pro : This thinner option is ideal for warmer climates where the primary goal is to reflect heat and prevent radiant heat gain.
  • 6mm Supreme : With its thicker design, the 6mm Supreme is perfect for colder climates or buildings that rely on heating systems regularly. The extra thickness provides enhanced insulation, making it better suited for retaining heat in cooler environments.

      2mm Pro : Features EPE foam , which is lightweight and flexible, providing sufficient insulation for warm-weather applications. 6mm Supreme : Uses XPE foam , which is denser and more robust, offering superior insulation and durability in buildings with extreme temperature swings or moisture issues.

    YES. You can COMBINE the Supreme 6mm on the roof and the PRO 2mm on the walls to provide great condensation control in colder climates at a lower cost, with minimal effect on heating costs and comfort.

    What if I plan to heat and cool my building consistently, like 24/7, so it's more like a livable space?

    Going fully conditioned with your metal building means you are heating/cooling the space 24/7 to maintain a constant temp at all times. Common applications for this install method include container living conversions, barndominiums/shouses, recreational/game rooms, and office spaces. If this is your plan for your building, then you need to combine the power of a radiant barrier with some r-value , using the amount recommended for your area. For this type of application we recommend the BlueTex™ Thermal Wrap if you don't already have a vapor barrier on the building; for building that already have a vapor barrier, our foil only radiant barrier is the right choice. More info on this type of install and products to use can be found here: Insulating Conditioned Buildings
