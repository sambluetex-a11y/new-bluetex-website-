Which BlueTex™ Insulation is Right For You?

Choose the 2mm Pro if:

    You live in a hot or moderate climate. Your building doesn’t rely on a heating system regularly (less than about 8 hours/week or not at all) You want a lightweight insulation option that’s easy to handle.
  • You live in a hot or moderate climate.
  • Your building doesn’t rely on a heating system regularly (less than about 8 hours/week or not at all)
  • You want a lightweight insulation option that’s easy to handle.

    • Choose the 6mm Supreme if:

      You live in a cold climate or experience harsh/wet winters. Your building requires regular heating (8+ hours a week but not full time 24/7). You need enhanced moisture resistance and greater insulation power.
  • You live in a cold climate or experience harsh/wet winters.
  • Your building requires regular heating (8+ hours a week but not full time 24/7).
  • You need enhanced moisture resistance and greater insulation power.

    • Choose the Radiant Barrier (or Foil/White) if:

      Your goal is heat rejection only - not looking for a vapor barrier. Your building is not heated/cooled. You want an economical way to cut down on the heat coming inside for an effect like putting the building in the shade.
  • Your goal is heat rejection only - not looking for a vapor barrier.
  • Your building is not heated/cooled.
  • You want an economical way to cut down on the heat coming inside for an effect like putting the building in the shade.

    • Choose the Thermal Wrap if:

      You are creating a fully conditioned space that is heated/cooled daily (like an office or a home). You are looking for a vapor barrier wrap product for new construction. You plan to use another insulation in addition to the radiant barrier, like spray foam or batt insulation for a higher R-value. You have a limited space to install and need a thinner product that is also a radiant barrier and vapor barrier.
  • You are creating a fully conditioned space that is heated/cooled daily (like an office or a home).
  • You are looking for a vapor barrier wrap product for new construction.
  • You plan to use another insulation in addition to the radiant barrier, like spray foam or batt insulation for a higher R-value.
  • You have a limited space to install and need a thinner product that is also a radiant barrier and vapor barrier.
