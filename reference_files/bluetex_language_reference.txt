BLUETEX LANGUAGE REFERENCE GUIDE
*** THIS DOCUMENT IS NOT FOR COPY/PASTE OR FINAL USE ***
*** THIS IS FOR LANGUAGE, TONE, AND STYLE REFERENCE ONLY ***

Source: BlueTex “Help Me Choose The Right Product” Guide

-------------------------------------
CORE BRAND LANGUAGE STYLE
-------------------------------------

- Educational, not salesy
- Speaks like an expert, not a marketer
- Focused on PROCESS, not just product
- Uses simple, practical explanations
- Assumes customer is trying to “solve a problem”
- Frequently compares real-world scenarios (ex: refrigerator analogy)

Example tone:
“We care about getting it right; we are not just here to sell products.”

-------------------------------------
KEY POSITIONING THEMES
-------------------------------------

1. PROCESS > PRODUCT
- Choosing the right PROCESS is just as important as the product
- Installation + use case matters more than specs alone

2. BUILDING USE DRIVES DECISION
- Always starts with:
  - What is your goal?
  - How are you using the building?

3. SIMPLICITY + CLARITY
- Avoid technical overload
- Break things into:
  - Conditioned vs Non-conditioned
  - Climate type
  - Usage frequency

-------------------------------------
COMMON LANGUAGE PATTERNS
-------------------------------------

Questions used to guide customer:

- “What’s your goal?”
- “How are you using the building?”
- “Are you heating/cooling it?”
- “How often are you using it?”

Customer scenarios:

- “Instant shade effect”
- “Prevent condensation / sweating”
- “Fully conditioned space”
- “Occasionally heated/cooled”

-------------------------------------
PRODUCT EXPLANATION STYLE
-------------------------------------

Each product is explained using:

1. What it DOES
2. When to USE it
3. What it’s BEST for
4. Real-world analogy

Example structure:

“Think of this product as…”
“This is best used when…”
“This is ideal for…”

-------------------------------------
SIMPLIFICATION STRATEGY
-------------------------------------

Breaks complex ideas into:

- Warm vs Cold climates
- Hours per week of heating/cooling
- Type of building (garage, barn, shop, etc.)

-------------------------------------
REPEATED VALUE PROPS
-------------------------------------

- Blocks 97% of radiant heat
- Vapor barrier (or not)
- Helps control condensation
- Improves comfort
- Clean white interior finish

-------------------------------------
ANALOGY STYLE (IMPORTANT)
-------------------------------------

Uses relatable comparisons:

Example:
“Think of your building like a refrigerator…”

Purpose:
- Makes technical concepts easy to understand
- Builds trust

-------------------------------------
BUYING GUIDANCE STYLE
-------------------------------------

- NOT pushy
- Recommends based on use case
- Encourages contacting for help
- Uses phrases like:

“If you’re not sure…”
“Contact us…”
“This depends on…”

-------------------------------------
HIERARCHY OF DECISION MAKING
-------------------------------------

1. Is the building conditioned?
2. How often is it used?
3. What climate?
4. Is condensation a concern?
5. Budget vs performance tradeoff
